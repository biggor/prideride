About Pride Ride

The Pride Ride is a 4-day, 600 km fundraising bike ride from Toronto to Montreal arriving the Friday of Montreal Pride Weekend.

Together, as volunteers, we commit to the physical and mental challenge of 600 km in 4 days.
We do this so that we can continue to remind people that HIV/AIDS is still very much with us, to help erase the stigma this disease still carries, to promote dignity and support for those living with HIV and AIDS, and to shout out loud - it’s time for AIDS to END.

Each person will cover their own costs during this journey. That means 100% of the funds we raise for HIV/AIDS charities will go to support the cause of ending AIDS.

We welcome everyone who shares our passion to see an end to this disease in our lifetime.

Come join with us.

Fundraising

The purpose of this ride is to continue the work of so many before us.
To this end we will ride with pride and raise much needed dollars for those charities working towards an end to HIV/AIDS. Each participant is free to choose their own charity or charities.

There is NO minimum fundraising requirement. Every dollar raised is a great dollar. There will be no ride expenses deducted from donations. Every dollar raised goes directly to your charity(s). Each participant will do what they can to support their charity or charities of choice.

While not exclusive we have included a list of suggested charities. Of course you are free to do your own research. Also included are links to sites that will give you accountability information on many of these charities: Charity Focus, Charity Intelligence Canada and Canada Revenue Agency.

Each participant will organise their own fundraising campaign, maintain a running total of donations, and keep the Ride updated so we can update the total amount on the web front page.

Frequently Asked Questions

When does registration open?

It’s open now.

What is the minimum age to participate?

18, or a letter from a parent or guardian

What is the registration process?

You e-bank or e-mail transfer $100 to the email address listed. You will receive an e-receipt for that money. Once registered you’ll be in a closed Facebook group where you can communicate and share with other participants.

If my $100 registration fee is not used up supporting the ride will I get a refund?

Yes. The $100 will go towards expenses like water, electrolyte powder, snacks, and vehicle rentals during the journey. There will be a full accounting of all group money spent, and if there is money leftover it will be divided among the participants and returned after the ride.

If I am financing this trip from my own pocket how much will it cost me?

We don’t know. What we do know is we will do whatever we can to keep costs low – group hotel rates, shared rooms, supply sponsors, donations given for support. We are hoping it will cost less, much less than $400 per participant inclusive of your registration fee.

What accommodation will we be offered?

We will be staying in motel/hotels for 3 nights. Hot showers with no lineups!! Mattresses!! Flush toilets!! We have a group rate at 2 motels in each location, one more upscale (swimming pool) than the other, both comfortable and decent. 2 people per room with room for more. We also have a very good group rate at a Village hotel in Montreal if you are staying the Friday arrival night and weekend.

I am a single person and don't know anyone else on the ride. Will I be able to room with someone to keep my costs down?

Yes. Once you arrive in the Facebook group you can start looking for room shares.

How many people can we get into a motel room to keep our costs low?

4

Will there be coffee and breakfast each morning on the road?

Yes, or someone will die a quick painful death.

What will road support supply riders with?

Love, encouragement, support, oh, and water, electrolytes, snacks, tire pumps, SAG (support and gear), and lots of cowbell. What they won’t do is change a flat tire.

Do we have to stay in motels? Can I tent camp?

Yes, but that’s up to you to arrange, let us know where you will be, and don’t get lost.

What if I have a mechanical breakdown on the road?

See “road support” above.

Will we ride in a pack?

No. Over these distances that’s impossible. Although you may find yourself riding alone at times most folks will find a pace group that suits them.

Is there a minimum I have to fundraise to be part of this ride?

No. We know you will do your utmost to raise as much as you can, and that is enough.

How will I get home from Montreal?

All participants will find their own return transportation. We will bring back the bikes unless you will be returning with yours or carrying on riding from Montreal.

If I am a Roadie do I have to pay my own costs on the road?

Only lodging and food, as will the riders.

Will the routes be marked and marshalled?

No. Each day you will receive a route map, and a GPS file if so desired. There will be a phone number to call if you get lost. Learn to read a map and don’t get lost.

Will there be restroom facilities along the way?

No. We pass through many towns, and Canada has plenty of trees and bushes.

What happens if I am in an accident?

God forbid that should happen. 911 will respond when called. The SAG vehicles will respond when called, and it’s a good idea to ride with someone(s). If you do not have a phone it’s a good idea to ride with someone who does.

Will there be training rides?

Once registered and in the Facebook group riders can share rides and training info. There will be several 160km+ rides planned closer to rideoff, and we’re thinking about a long weekend back to back long ride perhaps combined with a sleepover.

Will I need to train to ride this course?

For these distances? Hello!!

How long each day will I have to ride?

The road will open at 7am each morning and close at 6pm. A trained Iron rider will finish the 186km day in just over 6 hours. The rest of us mere mortals will take a bit longer. If you are not approaching the finish by 5:30pm you will be SAG’d in.

Pride Ride Registration


Registration is a commitment of $100.


This money may be used to provide support on the road. If at the finish there is money left over it will be returned to the participants. We will work as a group to manage expenses and keep the costs minimal.

The lowest cost way of registering is e-transfer or e-banking funds - usually $1.50. Send your registration to registration@prideride.ca and include your contact information in the “message” box of the transfer form.
