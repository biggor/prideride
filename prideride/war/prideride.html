About Pride Ride

The Pride Ride is a 4-day, 600km fundraising bike ride from Toronto to Montreal arriving the Friday of Montreal Pride Weekend.

We are a volunteer group of cyclists and crew committed to the physical and mental challenge of riding 600 km in 4 days. We want to create awareness that HIV/AIDS is still with us, to help erase the stigma this disease still carries, and to support an environment in which people living with HIV and AIDS can maintain their dignity.

Each person will cover their own costs during this journey. That means 100% of the funds we raise for HIV/AIDS charities will go to support the cause of ending AIDS.

We welcome everyone who shares our passion to see an end to this disease in our lifetime.

Come join us.

Fundraising

The purpose of this ride is to raise money for charities committed to ending AIDS. Each participant is free to choose their own charity or charities.

There is NO minimum and NO maximum fundraising requirement. Every dollar raised is a great dollar. There will be no ride expenses deducted from donations. 100% of all money raised goes directly to your charity(s).

Each participant will organize their own fundraising campaign, maintain a running total of donations, and keep the Ride updated so we can update the total amount on the web front page.

While not exclusive we have included a list of suggested charities. Of course you are free to do your own research. Also included are links to 3 sites that will give you accountability information on many of these charities: Charity Focus, Charity Intelligence Canada and Canada Revenue Agency.

Pride Ride Registration


Registration is a commitment of $100.


This money may be used to provide support on the road. If at the finish there is money left over it will be returned to the participants. We will work as a group to manage expenses and keep the costs minimal.

The lowest cost way of registering is e-transfer or e-banking funds - usually $1.50. Send your registration to gdahome@gmail.com and include your contact information in the “message” box of the transfer form.
